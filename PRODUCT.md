# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Competitive tennis players, their coaches, and team analysts: data-driven athletes at the club, collegiate, and aspiring-pro level. They already capture matches with ELC tools (Swingvision and similar) and have raw data, but it lives match-by-match in spreadsheets and clips. On this landing page they are evaluating whether Advantage is the serious, trustworthy home for that data. The context is calculated, not impulsive: a player between matches on a laptop, a coach scouting an opponent before a fixture. They self-serve and sign up for a free account.

## Product Purpose

Advantage is the centralized intelligence hub for competitive tennis. It ingests ELC/Swingvision match data and turns it into pro-grade output: multi-match aggregated trends, opponent scouting and weakness radars, custom performance metrics (e.g. a Consistency Index), AI-surfaced insights, and coach-ready PDF reports. It exists because elite-level analysis was previously locked to ATP/WTA analysts; Advantage makes it accessible to the club and collegiate player. Success is a free signup that turns into an active, returning analyst of their own game.

## Brand Personality

Precise, athletic, intelligent. The voice is authoritative and direct, the way a good coach is: it speaks in specifics and data, never hype. Three words: **precise, confident, earned**. The emotional goal on landing is *quiet confidence* — the visitor should feel their data is in serious, professional hands and that the people behind this respect the craft of the sport.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template.** No hero-metric cliche, no identical icon-heading-text card grids, no gradient-blob backgrounds, no stock illustration. If it could be any B2B tool, it has failed.
- **Overdesigned / loud.** No excessive animation, no glassmorphism-everywhere, no competing accent colors, no noise textures for their own sake. Restraint signals premium.
- Specifically avoid v0's three inconsistent blues, decorative glowing orbs, and typing-text gimmicks.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the product.** Every claim is backed by a real screenshot of the actual dashboard or report UI, never an abstract graphic. The product is the proof.
2. **Data over adjectives.** Use specific, quantified language and real metrics. Banned: "take your game to the next level."
3. **Quiet confidence.** Whitespace, restraint, and precision do the persuading. Loud is insecure.
4. **Earned motion.** Animation only when it reveals or clarifies data on scroll. Never decorative. Always honors reduced-motion.
5. **One blue.** A single committed accent (`#3B82F6`) carries every highlight, CTA, and focus state, exactly as the product does. Neutrals are tinted, never competing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: AA contrast on all text and CTAs, full keyboard navigation with visible focus rings, semantic HTML, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` path that disables scroll reveals and animation. Extra care on motion since the page uses scroll-triggered reveals.
